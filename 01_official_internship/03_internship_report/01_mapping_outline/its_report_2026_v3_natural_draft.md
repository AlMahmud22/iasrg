# CHAPTER 2: SPECIFIC DETAILS ON PROJECTS / TRAINING

## 2.1 Introduction

This chapter explains the main work completed during the industrial training period. The main project was the cyber fraud revictimization topic assigned by the supervisor. The work required reading, searching for suitable information, preparing weekly findings, and drafting early research paper sections.

The training also included Wazuh as a supporting task. It was useful for understanding practical cybersecurity monitoring, while the main training direction remained the cyber fraud research work.

## 2.2 Main Project Title

The main assigned project title was **Study on Identification of Factors and Development of Modules for Methods to Prevent Cyber Fraud Revictimization Problems Among Public in Malaysia**. The topic focused on understanding why people may become victims again after being exposed to cyber fraud and what factors may influence safer online behaviour.

The project required both cybersecurity understanding and behavioural understanding. Many scams depend on trust, urgency, social influence, fear, and poor verification habits, so the topic needed to be studied from both sides.

## 2.3 Work Completed

During the training, I studied cyber fraud analysis and information gathering. This included scam crime, scam types, latest trends, issues, challenges, and common techniques used by scammers.

I also prepared early research paper materials. I arranged sources, compared information, prepared weekly progress findings, and drafted early sections related to the problem background, theory, and research direction. The work helped me understand how a broad topic becomes clearer after the sources and arguments are organized properly.

For the Wazuh task, I gathered information about Wazuh, SIEM, XDR, SOC, and endpoint monitoring. I also attempted a local setup on my laptop and tested the basic environment to understand how the system works.

## 2.4 Hardware and Software Used

The main hardware used during the training was my laptop. The main software and materials used included web browsers, Microsoft Word, Markdown files, spreadsheet-style notes, Wazuh documentation, Wazuh setup files, academic papers, official reports, and cybersecurity references.

AI-assisted tools were used only as support for idea searching, comparison, and drafting. The information still needed to be checked and arranged properly before being used in the report.

## 2.5 Knowledge Applied

The training applied knowledge from computer network and security, cybersecurity awareness, technical reading, and report writing. The cyber fraud work helped me understand scam techniques, victim-related issues, and the importance of reliable sources.

The Wazuh task helped me connect the training with SOC, SIEM, XDR, endpoint monitoring, and alert concepts. It also gave me experience in learning a tool through documentation and basic testing.

## 2.6 Challenges

One challenge was that cyber fraud is a broad topic. At the beginning, many materials were related to general scam awareness, but not all of them were suitable for the assigned topic. I managed this by focusing on cyber fraud revictimization, victim behaviour, protection intention, and theory-related sources.

Another challenge was arranging the report materials clearly. Some information was useful as background only, while some was more suitable for the main discussion. I managed this by separating notes, references, and draft sections.

For Wazuh, the main challenge was understanding the different components and how they communicate. I handled this by reading the documentation, doing basic setup, and testing what could be done in my laptop environment.

## 2.7 Conclusion

The project and training tasks improved my understanding of cyber fraud research, source checking, report drafting, and cybersecurity monitoring tools. The main output was early research work on the cyber fraud topic, supported by Wazuh study and basic local testing.
