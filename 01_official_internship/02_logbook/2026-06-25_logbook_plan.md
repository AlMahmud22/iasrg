## 1. UTM Logbook Guidance, Translated and Applied

The logbook is written for monitoring and assessment by the faculty supervisor and organisation supervisor, while also helping the intern keep evidence of personal learning. In the UTM briefing, the logbook is part of the graded industrial training assessment and should be updated daily or weekly.

A good entry is not a diary. It should be a systematic, purposeful, and meaningful collection of work completed during training. Each working-day entry should show:

- Objective: the task or problem assigned, and the specific goal for that day.
- Activity: how the objective was attempted, including sources, tools, methods, comparisons, errors, or results.
- Achievement / Reflection: what was produced, what was learned, why a method was chosen, and what should be continued next.

Avoid:

- repeated sentences across many days;
- blog-style narration such as arrival time, lunch time, or personal travel details;
- vague lines such as "continued research" without explaining the exact research focus;
- claiming implementation or deployment when the work was only research, feasibility study, or planning.

Acceptable logbook content can include research operations, process notes, figures, tables, pseudocode, system diagrams, screenshots, source summaries, and short explanations of tools or systems used. AI-assisted search or comparison can be mentioned carefully when it was used for idea exploration, quick source discovery, or checking direction, but the entry should still show manual reading, understanding, summarising, and report writing as the main work.
