5.2.9 Discussion of Findings

This section will interpret the findings after data are available. The discussion should be organized around the research objectives:

(a) Objective 1: Identify psychosocial factors. Discuss which seven factors are strongest and how they compare with TPB, PMT, Cognitive Appraisal Theory, and Malaysian evidence.

(b) Objective 2: Develop intervention modules. Discuss whether the module design addresses the strongest risk factors and the weakest protective capabilities.

(c) Objective 3: Evaluate effectiveness. Discuss whether the intervention improves scenario performance, tool-use capability, reporting intention, recovery-scam resistance, and protective self-efficacy.

The discussion must not overclaim. If results are based on non-probability sampling, limited prior-victim numbers, or immediate post-test only, the limitations must be acknowledged. If repeat-victim data are insufficient for strong inference, this must be clearly stated.

5.3 Future Works

Future work should strengthen the study in four ways. First, future research should use a larger prior-victim and repeat-victim sample so that revictimization can be modelled with stronger statistical confidence. Second, future studies should include delayed follow-up to determine whether improvements remain after several weeks or months. Third, future research should compare intervention formats, such as classroom delivery, web-based training, mobile micro-learning, chatbot-based scam simulation, or gamified scenario practice. Fourth, future work should examine collaboration between banks, regulators, universities, community organisations, platform providers, and public agencies to deliver targeted revictimization prevention support.

The future work should remain connected to the study's findings once data are available. Any new recommendation should be based on measured weaknesses, such as low tool-use capability, high risky compliance in a specific scenario, low reporting intention, or strong shame-related avoidance among prior victims.
