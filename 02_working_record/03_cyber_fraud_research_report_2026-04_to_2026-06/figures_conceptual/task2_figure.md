This file prepares the figure information for `task_2_report.md`. It does not contain the generated images. It gives the figure names, suggested filenames, image style, purpose, and image generation prompts that can be used later to create the images.

The figure names below can be used as captions when the images are manually inserted into the DOCX file.

## General Image Generation Rules

- Use a mixed style: academic diagrams for conceptual figures and realistic or semi-realistic visuals for scam-example figures.
- Keep all images report-ready, clean, readable, and suitable for an academic research paper.
- Avoid real bank logos, real government logos, real agency logos, copyrighted app interfaces, real personal data, account numbers, QR codes, phone numbers, email addresses, and clickable-looking URLs.
- Keep in-image text minimal because generated text may be inaccurate. The final figure name and caption should be handled in the report, not inside the image.
- Use Malaysia-specific context through safe visual cues such as mobile banking, e-wallets, messaging apps, investment groups, public reporting channels, and urban Malaysian public settings.
- Do not include unsupported statistics, exact national loss figures, or numerical claims inside any image.
- Use neutral fictional interface elements and generic icons only.
- Use clear spacing, simple composition, and high contrast so that the figures remain readable after being inserted into DOCX.

## Figure 1 Brief

**Suggested filename:** `fig1.png`

**Figure name/caption:** Figure 1: Seven-factor framework for cyber fraud revictimization.

**Purpose:** Show the seven-factor taxonomy as the central backbone of the report. The figure should help readers quickly understand that revictimization is influenced by behavioural, social, emotional, financial, authority-based, urgency-based, and capability-based factors.

**Recommended image type:** Academic circular framework infographic.

**Image generation prompt:**

```text
Use case: infographic-diagram
Asset type: academic report figure
Primary request: Create a clean academic circular framework infographic showing seven connected factors around the central concept of cyber fraud revictimization among the public in Malaysia.
Scene/backdrop: Plain white or very light neutral background suitable for a thesis report.
Subject: A central shield or circular node representing cyber fraud revictimization, surrounded by seven evenly spaced factor nodes. The factors should be represented with simple generic icons: confidence gap, social proof, authority impersonation, urgency pressure, emotional recovery vulnerability, financial opportunity pressure, and verification/reporting capability.
Style/medium: Professional academic infographic, minimal vector-like illustration, clean lines, readable spacing.
Composition/framing: Landscape composition, centered circular model, balanced margins, enough space around all nodes for later captioning.
Lighting/mood: Calm, serious, professional, research-oriented.
Color palette: Neutral blue, teal, slate, and soft red accents for risk cues. Do not make it overly decorative.
Text: Minimal text only. Use short labels if needed: F1, F2, F3, F4, F5, F6, F7. Avoid long sentences.
Constraints: Make it suitable for a Malaysian cyber fraud research paper. Use generic symbols only. Do not use real logos, real agency names, real bank names, numbers, statistics, QR codes, or personal data.
Avoid: Crowded layout, tiny unreadable text, cartoon style, dramatic hacker imagery, fake statistics, official logos, real app screenshots, watermarks.
```

**Avoid/quality notes:**

- This figure should be conceptual, not a real-life scam scene.
- If text appears distorted, regenerate with only `F1` to `F7` labels and keep the detailed factor names in the report caption or surrounding text.
- Use this as the main overview figure in the report.
