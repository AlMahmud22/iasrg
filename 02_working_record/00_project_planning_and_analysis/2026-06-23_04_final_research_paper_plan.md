## 4. Main Missing Parts

### 4.1 Operational Definition of Revictimization

The final paper must clearly define what "revictimization" means.

It should not only mean "losing money twice." It can include:

- repeated scam exposure,
- repeated risky response,
- repeated contact by scammers,
- repeat financial loss,
- fake recovery scam targeting,
- delayed reporting after a first scam,
- continued vulnerability after prior victimization.

This definition must appear early in the paper and must connect to the measurement design.

### 4.2 Clear Mapping Between Two Seven-Factor Models

The lecturer's seven categories are:

1. Psychological factors
2. Behavioural factors
3. Socio-demographic factors
4. Technological factors
5. Environmental factors
6. Institutional factors
7. Temporal or time-related factors

The latest report uses these practical mechanisms:

1. Overconfidence
2. Social influence
3. Authority impersonation
4. Urgency and fear
5. Emotional grooming
6. Financial pressure
7. Protective capability

The final paper should use the lecturer's categories as the main academic framework, then place the practical mechanisms inside them. This is safer because it follows the task brief while keeping the useful practical model.

### 4.3 Source Verification

This is one of the biggest missing tasks.

Before writing the final paper, every important statistic and reference must be checked. Some older drafts use vendor blogs, news articles, broad web sources, and possibly inconsistent figures. These can be used carefully, but they should not carry the main argument.

Priority source order:

1. Official Malaysian sources
2. Academic journal articles
3. Legal and regulatory sources
4. News reports quoting official agencies
5. Vendor or security blogs for technical scam mechanisms only

Needed output before final writing:

- a clean evidence matrix,
- checked URLs,
- exact statistics,
- source credibility notes,
- source role in the paper.

### 4.4 Full Research Questions and Objectives

The current direction is good, but the final paper needs sharper research questions.

Possible final research questions:

1. What psychological, behavioural, socio-demographic, technological, environmental, institutional, and temporal factors influence cyber fraud revictimization risk among the Malaysian public?
2. How do TPB, PMT, and Cognitive Appraisal Theory explain protective intention, protective behaviour, and repeat scam vulnerability?
3. How can scenario-based assessment measure risky, partial, and safe scam responses?
4. How can a theory-based intervention module improve protective capability and reduce revictimization risk?

### 4.5 Full Measurement Instrument

The current files mention questionnaire areas, but the final paper needs actual items.

Missing items:

- demographic questions,
- prior victimization questions,
- repeat exposure questions,
- TPB items,
- PMT items,
- cognitive appraisal items,
- seven-factor items,
- reporting behaviour items,
- tool-use capability items,
- scenario response questions,
- intervention feedback items.

The instrument should include Likert-scale questions and scenario-based questions.

### 4.6 Scenario Tasks

Scenario tasks are important because they test behaviour, not just confidence.

Needed scenario examples:

- fake bank call or SMS,
- Macau scam authority impersonation,
- fake investment group,
- job scam,
- malicious APK delivery scam,
- fake parcel/customs payment,
- romance or emotional grooming scam,
- fake recovery service after a previous scam.

Each scenario should have:

- situation,
- user decision options,
- safe answer,
- partial answer,
- risky answer,
- scoring rule,
- factor being tested.

### 4.7 Sampling and Ethics

The paper needs a clearer sampling and ethics plan.

Needed details:

- target population,
- age range,
- Malaysian public/digital users,
- prior victims and non-victims,
- attempted-scam exposed users,
- sample size target,
- recruitment method,
- consent statement,
- privacy protection,
- no collection of bank details, passwords, IC numbers, or real scam evidence unless formal approval exists.

### 4.8 Data Analysis Plan

The final paper needs a stronger analysis plan.

Possible analysis:

- descriptive statistics,
- reliability testing such as Cronbach's alpha,
- validity checks,
- correlation analysis,
- regression or PLS-SEM,
- pre-test and post-test comparison,
- scenario safe-response score comparison,
- group comparison between prior victims and non-victims.

### 4.9 Intervention Module Details

The current intervention idea is good, but it needs more operational detail.

Suggested module structure:

1. Scam and revictimization awareness
2. Scam cue detection
3. Threat and coping appraisal
4. Verification and reporting routine
5. Recovery support and fake recovery scam resistance

Each module should include:

- learning objective,
- theory link,
- factor addressed,
- activity,
- scenario practice,
- expected behaviour change,
- evaluation method.

## 5. Do We Need to Create Software?

Not immediately.

For the research paper itself, software is not mandatory if the final deliverable is a proposal or conceptual research paper. A paper-based module, survey, and scenario instrument can be enough.

However, a small software prototype would be useful if the final project wants to show "development of modules" in a practical and testable way.

Recommended decision:

- If the requirement is only a research paper: do not build software yet.
- If the requirement includes module development and testing: build a small prototype after the research design is finalized.
- If the lecturer expects a practical output: build a simple web-based training and assessment tool.

## 8. Suggested Final Paper Structure

Based on the UTM template, the final paper should use this chapter backbone:

1. CHAPTER 1 INTRODUCTION
   - Introduction
   - Problem Background
   - Research Aim
   - Research Questions
   - Research Objectives
   - Research Scope
   - Research Contribution
   - Report Organization

2. CHAPTER 2 LITERATURE REVIEW
   - Cyber fraud and scam crime
   - Cyber fraud trends in Malaysia
   - Scam types and techniques
   - Cyber fraud revictimization
   - TPB, PMT, and Cognitive Appraisal Theory
   - Seven categories of revictimization factors
   - Current initiatives and limitations
   - Research gap

3. CHAPTER 3 RESEARCH METHODOLOGY
   - Research design
   - Operational framework/research workflow
   - Conceptual framework
   - Variables and constructs
   - Instrument design
   - Sampling strategy
   - Data collection procedure
   - Data analysis method
   - Ethical considerations
   - Performance measurement

4. CHAPTER 4 RESEARCH DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION
   - Proposed solution
   - Intervention module design
   - Scenario-based assessment design
   - Questionnaire and scoring implementation
   - Prototype design if required
   - Testing method

5. CHAPTER 5 RESULTS, ANALYSIS AND DISCUSSION
   - Respondent/profile summary
   - Reliability and validity results
   - Seven-factor results
   - Scenario response results
   - Module evaluation results
   - Discussion
   - Future works

6. CHAPTER 6 CONCLUSION
   - Achievement of project objectives
   - Research contributions
   - Limitations
   - Suggestions for improvement and future works

References and appendices should come after the chapters. Front matter such as declaration, dedication, acknowledgement, abstract, table of contents, and lists can be prepared after the main chapter content is stable.
